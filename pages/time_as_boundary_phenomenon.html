Time as an Emergent Boundary

Inspired by the paper's Section 5.3, this illustrates time not as a fixed dimension, but as a phenomenon emerging from the **observation of change** within a conceptual space. Blue points represent potential or unobserved "Events." The dynamic orange/yellow line is the **"Present Moment" boundary**.

Time is the perceived passage as this boundary moves through the space of events. Events the boundary has passed are in the "Past" (grey), representing observed change. Events ahead are in the "Future" (blue), representing potential.

**Drag the boundary horizontally** to simulate the continuous emergence of time through the act of observation and differentiation between past and future.
